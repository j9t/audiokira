About Audiokira

Audiokira removes recurring frequency patterns—like electrical hum, HVAC noise, or tonal interference—from audio and video files, entirely in your browser.

How to Use Audiokira

  1. Load a file. Drop any audio or video file onto the page. The audio track is extracted automatically.
  2. Read the spectrogram. Horizontal bands that persist across the full width are recurring frequencies. The Y axis runs from 20 Hz (bottom) to the file’s Nyquist frequency (top) on a logarithmic scale; brighter color means louder.
  3. Add filters. Click anywhere on the spectrogram to add a filter at that frequency immediately. Or use “Detect recurring bands” to auto-add all persistent tonal frequencies at once. Presets cover common mains hum and bass/treble cuts.
  4. Preview. Click “Preview” to hear the result in real time. Filter changes apply immediately without stopping playback.
  5. Optional: Select a time range. Shift-drag on the waveform to limit filtering to a specific section. “Preview” plays the selection; “Export” applies filters only within it.
  6. Export. Choose WAV (lossless) or MP3, then click “Export.”

Limitations

  • Very large files may take several seconds to analyze.
  • Notch filters remove a frequency everywhere in the signal—they cannot distinguish unwanted noise from intentional sound at the same pitch. Use “Noise only” to hear what is removed, then adjust Q and preview normally to minimize collateral effect.
  • Spectrogram analysis uses the left (or mono) channel for visualization; export processes all channels.
  • Keyboard navigation for spectrogram interaction and waveform selection is lacking; while the tool ideally should be accessible to everyone, with only one user (added during early development) this isn’t currently being pursued.

Tips

  • Power line hum sits at 50 Hz (Europe) or 60 Hz (North America) and repeats at each harmonic—100/120, 150/180 Hz, and so on. The presets add all three at once.
  • “Detect recurring bands” flags frequencies present in more than roughly a third of the file.
  • Hovering over the spectrogram shows the frequency and nearest musical note. Clicking snaps to the strongest nearby peak; dragging vertically defines a band—the height of the drag sets the notch width (Q). The spectrogram can also be resized vertically by dragging its bottom-right corner.
  • “Filtered out only” plays back exactly what the filters remove—useful for verifying nothing intentional is being cut. Export always produces the cleaned signal regardless of this toggle.
  • A/B slots store two independent filter sets; clicking “A” or “B” saves the current set and loads the other for quick comparison.
  • The Gain slider compensates for volume loss from heavy filtering and is reflected in the export.
  • Use “Save current filters as preset…” to store a filter set in your browser for reuse across sessions.
  • Log/Linear switches the spectrogram Y axis—linear makes evenly-spaced hum harmonics easier to spot.
  • Use Ctrl + Z / Ctrl + Y (or Ctrl + Shift + Z) to undo and redo filter changes.
  • MP3 export requires an Internet connection to load the encoding library.

Privacy

Audiokira runs entirely in your browser. No audio, video, or any other data is uploaded to a server or shared with third parties. Files are read locally and don’t leave your device.
